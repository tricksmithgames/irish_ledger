Set your alarm. Tell the boss you might be a little slow on Friday morning. Ireland have a World Cup to get to.

On Thursday, 26 March, the Republic of Ireland travel to Prague to face Czechia in a FIFA World Cup 2026 play-off semi-final — a game that could put us on a plane to North America for the first time since Japan and Korea in 2002. For an entire generation of Irish fans abroad, this is the match we've been waiting for.

## What's at Stake

The format is simple, the stakes enormous. Beat Czechia in Prague, and Ireland host the second leg — a home play-off final — at the Aviva Stadium on Tuesday, 31 March against either Denmark or North Macedonia. Win that, and we're going to the World Cup. Ireland would land in Group A alongside co-hosts Mexico, South Africa, and South Korea.

Ireland last qualified for the World Cup when Mick McCarthy's side reached the quarter-finals in 2002. For anyone under 35, a World Cup with Ireland has been a dream, not a memory. That changes this week — or it doesn't, and we wait again.

## How to Watch From Abroad

**RTÉ Player** (rte.ie/player) will carry the full broadcast, and RTÉ2 has live coverage from 7:00 PM. Access from abroad may require a VPN set to an Irish server — this is the standard workaround for diaspora viewers and widely used. RTÉ Radio 1 will also carry live commentary from 6:00–10:00 PM.

In the **United States and Canada**, check ESPN+ and Peacock, both of which have carried UEFA qualifying coverage this cycle. In the **UK**, check ITV and ITVX, which typically broadcast Irish international matches.

Your local **GAA club or Irish pub** is also worth checking — from Boston to Sydney, Irish venues usually put on big screens for international matches, and there is no bigger occasion in Irish football this decade.

## The Road That Got Us Here

Ireland are in Prague because of one extraordinary man having the week of his life. In November 2025, Troy Parrott scored a hat-trick in Budapest — including a 96th-minute winner — to seal a dramatic 3-2 comeback victory over Hungary and clinch Ireland's play-off place. He had already starred in a stunning 2-0 defeat of world number one Portugal earlier in the campaign, when Cristiano Ronaldo was shown a red card and Ireland produced one of their greatest ever results.

Parrott's five goals in the November break turned what looked like another qualifying near-miss into something real. Ireland finished second in Group F and are now ninety minutes away from Prague from history.

## The Manager: Hallgrímsson Commits to the Journey

Manager Heimir Hallgrímsson announced his 25-man squad for the play-off alongside the news that he has signed a contract extension to remain as Ireland head coach through the Euro 2028 campaign. The Icelandic manager, who has brought a quiet confidence and tactical discipline to the squad, told reporters the FAI's "big pressure" persuaded him to extend. For Irish fans everywhere, it is a welcome sign of stability regardless of Thursday's result.

The squad includes the welcome return of Alan Browne, Robbie Brady, and Sammie Szmodics, while Chelsea midfielder Harvey Vale receives his first senior call-up. Josh Cullen is the only confirmed absentee through injury.

## The Opponents

Czechia are ranked 44th in the world — 16 places above Ireland — and their home record is formidable, with 15 wins from their last 18 games at the Fortuna Arena in Prague. They are not to be underestimated. But Ireland have beaten teams they had no right to beat before, and the atmosphere when the first notes of Amhrán na bhFiann ring out in Czech air will be something special.

Reports from Prague suggest hundreds of Irish fans have already made the trip, with flights and hotels sold out across the city. The green army travels.

## If Ireland Win

A home final at the Aviva on 31 March. A chance to roar Ireland to a World Cup in front of a packed Dublin crowd. If you're within striking distance of Ireland on 31 March and we get there — start looking at flights now.

And then in the summer of 2026, the World Cup itself. Mexico, South Africa, South Korea. Cities still to be confirmed. Pubs to be found. Flags to be flown.

Let's go.
