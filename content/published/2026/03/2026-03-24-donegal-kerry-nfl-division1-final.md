The weekend ahead has a lot going for it if you're Irish abroad. Thursday brings Ireland's World Cup play-off in Prague. Then, before you've had time to recover, there's the small matter of Donegal and Kerry locking horns in the Allianz Football League Division 1 Final.

Two of the most storied counties in Gaelic football. Two of the biggest diaspora communities in the world. One game, this weekend. Here is everything you need to know.

## How They Got Here

The road to the final provided plenty of drama. On Sunday, 22 March, Donegal defeated Monaghan 3-20 to 3-16 in what was described as an intense and free-flowing encounter. Kerry, meanwhile, saw off Armagh 0-24 to 1-21 — a high-scoring affair befitting a county with their tradition of attacking football.

The final weekend of the league also brought significant news for supporters of other counties: Dublin were relegated to Division 2 for 2027. For Kerry and Donegal fans, that's context worth savouring — the old order has shifted, and this final carries extra weight as a statement of intent heading into the championship summer.

## Why This Matters to the Diaspora

Donegal and Kerry each boast some of the most active and passionate GAA communities outside of Ireland. In the United States, the Donegal diaspora is concentrated in cities like New York (home to a historic Donegal GAA community in the Bronx), Philadelphia, and Boston. Kerry's people are scattered from the Kerry Men's Association of New York to the Kerry clubs of London and Melbourne.

In Chicago, where the Irish community is among the largest in the world, both county associations have proud histories stretching back generations. In Australia — particularly Melbourne and Sydney — Donegal and Kerry clubs have competed in county boards for decades.

For families with roots in the northwest — Letterkenny, Donegal Town, Ballyshannon, Glencolmcille — or the southwest — Tralee, Killarney, Dingle, Kenmare — this is a final to gather around, wherever you are in the world.

## When and Where to Watch

The Division 1 Final takes place this **weekend, 28–29 March**, at Croke Park in Dublin. Check the GAA's official site (gaa.ie) for the confirmed day and time as the week progresses.

**Watching from abroad:**

**GAAGO** (gaago.ie) is the primary streaming service for international GAA viewers and is likely to carry the final. GAAGO is available as a pay-per-game or subscription service outside of Ireland and is the most reliable option for watching live GAA from the US, UK, Australia, and beyond.

**TG4** (tg4.ie/en/player/) streams many league and championship matches and is available outside Ireland on its player — worth checking first, as league finals sometimes appear there for free.

**GAA+ Season Pass**: If you're a committed GAA follower abroad and haven't already, the GAA+ Season Pass is worth considering. In Ireland it's priced at €95; international pricing is available via gaaplus.ie, and covers over 180 matches per year for overseas subscribers including championship games. Individual matches can also be bought for €15, or three for €30.

**Your local GAA club** is always worth a call. From Boston to Birmingham, from New York to New South Wales, Irish clubs often put on big-screen showings for league and championship finals, and it's a much better craic than watching alone in a flat.

## A Bit of History

Both counties know what a league title means. Donegal, under Jim McGuinness, had one of the great modern eras in Ulster football, winning the All-Ireland in 2012. Their current team has been building impressively in recent seasons and this final appearance is reward for a confident league campaign.

Kerry need no introduction. The Kingdom is the most decorated county in GAA football history, with eight Sam Maguire titles in the modern era and a tradition of producing footballers of the highest quality. They will start as many people's favourites on Sunday.

But Donegal, under their current management, have the tactical shape and the firepower to cause problems. And they have the northwest behind them.

## The Hurling Finals Too

The same weekend brings the Allianz Hurling League Finals. If you're settling in for a GAA marathon — and honestly, why wouldn't you be — check gaa.ie for the full schedule of hurling action across the divisions. It is shaping up to be a genuinely great GAA weekend.

Whether you're watching from a pub in the Bronx, a living room in London, or a club bar in Melbourne, enjoy the game. And if your county is in it — bring the noise.
